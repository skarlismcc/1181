Grading Policies & Info

This class will focus on writing as a process, including multiple revisions before your Final Portfolio. Therefore, up until the Final Portfolio, all assignments (except for the quizzes) will be graded on a check system, with the focus on completing all required parts of the assignment.

I want you to focus on doing the work, not on worrying about your grade. So, if you complete the requirements and turn in all the required parts of the assignment, you will get full credit. Missing requirements will lead to partial credit, or if several requirements are missed, no credit.

At a Glance

  • Assignments (except Final Portfolio) graded on a check system (✓+, ✓, ✓−, or NC).
  • Weekly Writing & smaller tasks: partial-check scale recorded as 2 / 1 / 0.
  • Major projects: full-check scale recorded as 3 / 2 / 1 / 0.
  • Final Portfolio: graded with a 100-point rubric in Canvas.

Grading Policies

  • No resubmissions of graded work.
  • No pre-grading by email: feedback is built into class activities and the drafting process.
  • Use class time to get feedback on brainstorming, drafting, and revisions.
  • For additional feedback, make an appointment with a writing tutor at the Academic Success Center.

Partial Check Grading Scheme

For Weekly Writing Assignments and Supporting Work

✓+ Check Plus (✓ +) — Full credit Meets requirements: the assignment meets the required length, prompts, and any other requirements on the assignment sheet.
✓− Check minus (✓ -) — Partial credit Missing a requirement (or two, if both are minor). The assignment mostly meets the requirements but may be under the length requirement or does not address a required item.
NC No Credit (NC) Missing two or more requirements or substantially under the length requirement.

Full Check Grading Scheme

For Major Projects and the Macomb Scavenger Hunt

✓+ Check Plus (✓ +) — Full credit Meets length, style, formatting, and prompt requirements. Complete, well organized, generally well written and thoughtful.
✓ Check (✓ ) — Partial credit Missing one required item, under page requirements, ideas not fully developed, not detailed, or missing support or other requirements.
✓− Check minus (✓ -) — Partial credit Missing two required items (e.g., ideas not fully developed, under page requirements, and/or not addressing all prompts).
NC No Credit (NC) Missing three or more items, did not complete the assignment (off-topic or missing much of the work), and/or substantially under the page length requirements.
Note: if the assignment significantly does not meet requirements (e.g., completely off-topic or far under length), it may receive NC on that basis alone.

Incomplete Grade Policy

An Incomplete (I) grade may be granted only in exceptional circumstances and is not guaranteed. In this course, an Incomplete is reserved for students who have been consistently performing well throughout the semester and experience an unexpected, documented emergency near the end of the term that prevents them from submitting the final portfolio.

To be eligible for an Incomplete:

  • The student must have completed all major assignments leading up to the final portfolio.
  • The student must be passing the class at the time of the request.
  • The final portfolio must be the only outstanding work remaining in the course.
  • The student must contact the instructor as soon as possible to discuss the situation and provide appropriate documentation.

If an Incomplete is granted, a written agreement will outline the timeline and requirements for submitting the final portfolio. Failure to meet the agreed-upon deadline may result in a failing grade for the course.
