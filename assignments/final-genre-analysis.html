Steps for Revision

  1. Read through/listen to the comments that I left on your revised genre analysis draft from Project 1.
  2. If you need to get any additional examples from your discourse community, do it at the start of the revision (not later).
  3. Create a plan for revision. Identify major areas (high/middle-order concerns) first, then minor areas (later-order concerns).
  4. Use the revision resources on this website to help with revision or to get help with certain areas of your paper.
  5. Revise your draft. Expect a non-linear process with back-and-forth between samples and your paper; plan for substantial work.
  6. When revision is done, proofread your paper.
    • Consider installing the free version of Grammarly for Chrome so it can check directly on your paper (works on lab computers if you sign into Chrome).
    • You may use GenAI (e.g., Claude or ChatGPT) to help with proofreading; be cautious so it doesn’t change your voice or ideas.

I also highly recommend visiting the Academic Success Center for revision suggestions. Remember, you are not just “proofreading” your paper; you’re expected to do substantial revision work.

Grading

This paper must be revised since your revised draft for Project 1 (the revision after peer review), and it will be graded on demonstrating mastery of the course concepts and writing quality, along with meeting the original assignment criteria. The full rubric can be found on Canvas; a summary of the rubric is below.

Final Genre Analysis Essay — Rubric (Summary)

This is a simplified summary. Read the full rubric on Canvas for detailed descriptors and scoring ranges.

Genre Analysis Essay Requirements

50 pts
  • Meets all minimum requirements for the assignment.
  • Addresses prompts; 1300–1600 words (excludes Works Cited).
  • MLA format with in-text citations + Works Cited.
  • Uses at least three community writing samples as evidence.
  • Claims supported by samples and/or specific observations.
  • Primary research only (no secondary research).

Paper-level Organization

5 pts
  • Logical overall structure; parts appear where readers expect them.

Paragraph-level Organization

10 pts
  • Clear topic focus; sufficient development and coherence within paragraphs.

Flow, Transitions, Readability

5 pts
  • Ideas move smoothly; effective transitions; readable sentences.

Academic Style, Approach, and Tone

5 pts
  • Consistent academic voice and conventions; objective, appropriate tone.

Genre Samples and Evidence

10 pts
  • Integrates ≥3 samples; connects evidence to claims and analysis.

Formatting and MLA

5 pts
  • Correct MLA throughout; accurate Works Cited and citations.

Revision and Proofreading

10 pts
  • Substantive revisions from draft; few to no errors.
Total: 100 pts
