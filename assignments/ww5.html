Weekly Writing 5: Analyzing Genre

Similar to last week, choose one communication event (a post, video, written comment, etc.) from your community and answer the questions below. This is not an essay assignment—answers can be listed.

Reqirements

Length: Varies
Genres: Link or Screenshot; Responses to Questions

Instructions

  1. Pick one message/communication from your community. This could be a video post, a written post, a comment/reply, or something similar.
  2. Public Communities Only:
    • For example, TikTok, Instagram, Reddit, etc.
    • Link to the post by copying and pasting the link into your Weekly Writing 5 document.
    • Double-check that the link works.
  3. Closed/Private Communities Only:
    • For example, Discord servers, private Facebook groups, etc.
    • Take a screenshot of the post and copy/paste it into your Weekly Writing 5 document.
    • Then blur or black out all identifying information (usernames, names, photos).
    • Check the group’s rules about screenshots. If screenshots aren’t allowed, either ask permission or copy/paste the text from the post or comment.
  4. Answer the questions below your screenshot/link. (List format is fine; you do not need to write this as an essay.)

Questions to Answer

  • What community is this from?
  • What is the genre of your communication event?
    For example: TikTok video, YouTube video, Discord message, Reddit post, etc.
  • If you needed to get more specific, what would you call it?
    For example: GymTok instructional video, tutorial video, review video, game-review post, POV video, “Getting Ready With Me” video, etc. If your genre is something like a comments section or general Discord chat, you might not be able to get more specific—just note that.
  • What do you notice about how this post/message is written or created? Focus on rhetorical moves rather than content.
    Possible things to consider: camera angles/positioning, music (is there music? what kind?), color choices, casual vs. formal tone, text formatting, captions, images, emoji use, organization of information, etc.
  • Are the features you noticed above common in this type of post/message in your community? Are most communications like this, or different? In what ways?

Grading

✓+ (2/2)
All requirements for the assignment are met, including word count minimums, prompts, clearly referencing the reading (when applicable).
✓- (1/2)
One of the above criteria is not met.
NC (0/2)
(no credit)
Two or more of the above criteria are not met.
