Peer Review Instructions

  • Leave feedback on the paper using Canvas (see instructions below).
  • Leave at least one comment that:
    • addresses a high- or middle-order concern that could use improvement (organization, details, paragraph structure, examples, flow, transitions, etc.).
    • points to a specific spot in the paper that illustrates the issue.
  • Leave at least one comment about something that’s working well (ideally connected to a high-/middle-order concern).
  • Do not leave later-order concerns (grammar/spelling/mechanics) unless they block readability. If so, make one note to explain the issue.
  • Do not edit your classmate’s draft (no direct grammar fixing in the text).

Additional Information

  • Focus on ideas that could improve the paper. You don’t have to prescribe exact fixes.
  • It’s fine to comment as a reader—describe what you notice rather than trying to “be the editor.”
  • Use feedback sandwiches when possible (from the “In-Class Peer Review” reading).
  • Leave only high- and middle-order concern feedback.
    • No grammar/spelling feedback.
    • Exception: For a consistent issue (e.g., fragments/run-ons) that hurts readability or flow, you can mark it once to flag the trend.
  • For in-person classes: you may discuss papers verbally, but make sure feedback is also left on the draft in Canvas.

High- and Middle-Order Concerns

High-Order Concerns: The Big Stuff

Think about assignment requirements, overall organization, audience, purpose, and genre.

  • Does the paper meet the assignment prompts and use evidence from genre samples?
  • Is it written as an academic analysis essay for an appropriate audience?
  • Do ideas appear in a logical order, and does the paper stay on topic?

Leave feedback on any area that could be improved.

Middle-Order Concerns: The Effective Writing Stuff

  • Are paragraphs well-developed (e.g., Topic-Evidence-Discussion)? Are claims supported with quotes, descriptions, or screenshots from genre samples?
  • Are there transitions connecting ideas? Does the draft feel choppy or disconnected?
  • Any sentences that are too long, awkward, or hard to read?

Leave feedback on any area that could be improved.

Instructions for Using Canvas for Peer Review

  1. Go to Modules and open the assignment titled Rough Draft with Peer Review.
  2. Click one of your assigned peer reviews.
  3. Click View Feedback.
  4. Use the double-sided arrows to expand the document to full screen.
  5. Use the annotation tools (highlighter, comment tool) to leave feedback on the draft.

Viewing Peer Review Feedback

  • Open the assignment in Modules → Rough Draft with Peer Review.
  • Your peer’s summary comment appears in the comments panel on the right.
  • To view annotations on the paper, click View Feedback. Comments should appear alongside your draft.
