Project 1 — Genre Analysis Essay Revised Draft

Your draft must be effectively revised after you receive feedback from Peer Review (or the ACS tutor). This revision should primarily focus on high and middle order concerns, such as organization, flow, transitions, use of evidence, and paragraph development. While proofreading (i.e., fixing typos and mechanics) is appreciated, that alone will not fulfill the revision requirement.

The revised draft must also meet all other requirements for the paper, which are listed on this assignment sheet, such as word count requirements, citations, formatting, using evidence, and covering the required topics.

Revision Requirements

  • Length: 1300–1600 words (~5–7 pages) not including the Works Cited page.
  • Format: MLA Style.
  • Content: Address all required prompts/topics listed under the "Assignment Prompt" section on the Genre Analysis Essay assignment page.
  • Submission: Submit to Canvas as a Word document (.doc or .docx).

Evidence & Analysis Guidelines

  • Samples: Use at least three writing/video samples from your community to support your points.
    For shorter genres, such as Discord messages, I recommend using a series of messages from three separate conversations.
  • Visuals & Quotes: Include direct quotes and/or screenshots (for visual conventions) as support.
  • Support: Support your claims throughout your paper using either your genre samples or specific details from your observations in your community.
  • Trends: Use more than one sample/example to support each convention since you are trying to show a trend.

Citations & Integrity

  • In-text Citations: Include in-text citations (i.e., clearly state the username/name of the creator of the post/message that you are analyzing).
  • Works Cited: Include a Works Cited page (MLA Style) with links/references to your samples.
    See the MLA Formatting & Citations page for more information.
  • AI Statement: A detailed AI Statement of Use is required at the end of your paper.
    If you did not use GenAI in any capacity, note that in the statement of use.

Grading Criteria for Revised Draft

✓+
(3/3) All above requirements are met.
✓
(2/3) Missing one of the above requirements.
✓-
(1/3) Missing two or three of the above requirements and/or the paper is substantially under the word count requirement.
NC
No Credit (0/3) Missing four or more of the above requirements.

Note: Assignments that are missing major criteria will be graded accordingly. For example, if the paper is significantly off-topic, incomplete, and/or substantially under the word count may result in a no credit grade.
