Weekly Writing 4: Analysis of a Communication Event

For this week, choose one communication event (a post, video, written comment, etc.) from your community and answer the questions below. This is not an essay assignment—answers can be listed.

Reqirements

Length: Varies
Genres: Link or Screenshot; Responses to Questions

Instructions

  1. Pick one message/communication from your community. This could be a video post, a written post, a comment/reply, or something similar.
  2. Link to the post (public communities only) or include a screenshot. Double-check that the link works. To link, just paste the URL from your browser into your document.
  3. For screenshots, blur or black out all identifying information (usernames, names, photos).
    • This is especially important for closed/private communities (e.g., Discord servers, private Facebook groups).
    • Check the group’s rules about screenshots. If screenshots aren’t allowed, either ask permission or copy/paste the text from the post or comment.
  4. Paste the screenshot or link into your Weekly Writing 4 document.
  5. Answer the following questions below your screenshot/link. (List format is fine.)

Questions to Answer

  • What community is this from? (e.g., “This linked video from the BookTok community.”)
  • What is the message/post about?
  • What do you think the purpose of this message/post is?
  • Who do you think the primary audience is? What about the post makes you think that?
  • Is this post/message a typical type of communication in your community?
  • Does the post/message use any language that is (or might be) specific to this community? If so, what?
  • Did others interact with the post/message? If so, how many, and how did they interact? (For example: “493 people liked this post; 283 commented.”)

Grading

✓+ (2/2)
All requirements for the assignment are met, including word count minimums, prompts, clearly referencing the reading (when applicable).
✓- (1/2)
One of the above criteria is not met.
NC (0/2)
(no credit)
Two or more of the above criteria are not met.
