MLA Style from the Modern Language Association is a standard for how to format your paper and cite sources. In ENGL 1181, we use MLA conventions common to the humanities.

MLA Style Formatting

  • Double-space the entire paper; use a clear 11–12 pt font (e.g., Times New Roman, Calibri, Arial).
  • Set 1″ margins on all sides.
  • Indent the first line of each paragraph by 0.5″ (use the Tab key).
  • Heading at upper-left on page 1: your name, instructor’s name, course, date. No cover page.
  • Running header (LastName space page#) in the upper-right on pages 2+.
  • Provide in-text citations for all quoted, paraphrased, and summarized material.
  • If you use endnotes, place them on a separate page before the Works Cited.
  • Include a Works Cited page; entries should match your in-text citations and use a hanging indent.

Additional Writing Guidelines

  • If an author’s gender is unknown, use the singular “they.” MLA guidance
  • Favor precise, clear wording and vocabulary you are comfortable using while developing academic voice.
  • For fuller guidance, see the MLA Handbook (9th ed.) and the MLA Online Guide.

In-Text Citations

Two common patterns:

  • Author mentioned in the sentence → add page number in parentheses if available.
    Example: Smith notes similar conventions in a related video (14).
  • Parenthetical reference → put author’s last name and page number at the end of the sentence.
    Example: The preview video includes a long caption with the release date (Jones).

Basic In-Text Citation Format

(Author’s Last Name Page#) — e.g., (Smith 14). If the author is already named in the sentence, include only the page number in parentheses.

Since you'll be referencing social media posts or comments instead of books or traditional sources, your citations will look a little different (see below).

Social Media In-Text Citations

Use the account username; for Facebook, use the account name.

If you include the name/username in the sentence, don’t repeat it in parentheses.

Examples

A video by @CrazyShook39 includes a caption with the release date and purchase links.

The caption lists the release date and retailers (@CrazyShook39).

Works Cited Page

Your Works Cited page is the last page of your paper; list entries alphabetically with a hanging indent. A typical social-media entry looks like:

Author and/or username. “Post/Caption/Title.” Platform, Date, URL.

Examples

@Tiiffxny. “Halter tops worn backwards are now my favorite thing??? What item of clothes should I style next??” YouTube Shorts, 7 Oct. 2022, https://www.youtube.com/shorts/cZ5vgfiFP84

u/douglonious. “My water currently here in central Texas.” Reddit, 27 Sept. 2024, https://www.reddit.com/r/Wellthatsucks/comments/1fqc9c0/…
