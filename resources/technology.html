Purchase & Loaner Program

To purchase a laptop or tablet, you can either:

  • Purchase on your own, or
  • Purchase through the bookstore.

Students receiving financial aid can use financial aid to purchase course materials and necessary supplies (including laptops).

Purchase Options & Considerations

For our class, a tablet, laptop, or Chromebook will all work. However, consider your major and what you might need down the road before purchasing anything.

Laptop (Windows or Mac)

A standard laptop will likely run any software you might need during college.

If you are going into a tech-heavy field, such as graphic design, animation, or computer science, you may want to talk to a professor in that department before making a decision.

Tablet with Keyboard (iPad or Android)

For many of your classes, a tablet with a keyboard will likely be enough. A tablet also can provide a better experience for reading and highlighting online and digital textbooks. However, not all software is available for tablets, and you may find some functionality limited.

Chromebook

Depending on your major and the software needed, a Chromebook may also work. You will be able to use Office 365 online and Google Docs / Suite online for most of your work. These laptops can also be much less expensive than a standard laptop. However, if you need to install specific software, you might not have that option on a Chromebook.

Accessing Microsoft Office 365

Macomb students receive free access to Microsoft Office 365 (including Word, PowerPoint, Excel, and Teams).

  • Login: office.com
  • Use your Macomb student email and password.
  • You can use Office 365 apps online or install them on your device.

Microsoft Teams

Microsoft Teams is used for virtual meetings, online office hours, and collaboration. You can access Teams at microsoft.com / teams / log-in.

Download the desktop or mobile app for the best experience.
