Analyzing Magazines and Ads

Part 1: Looking at the whole magazine

Learn everything you can about the intended audience.

  • Who is the audience?
  • How can we tell?

Look at ads, articles, photos—anything that gives clues.

Part 2: Analyzing an advertisement

Consider: color, images, objects, people, expressions, words, language choice, font choice, mood/tone, symbols, etc. Write each on your paper and explain how it targets the audience.

  • Who is the audience for this ad?
  • How can you tell?

Example (color): “The colors are bright and attention-grabbing with lots of pinks, purples, and blues—skewing feminine/young to match the audience.”

Be thoughtful and detailed. If there’s time, swap magazines and do this twice. Be prepared to share.
